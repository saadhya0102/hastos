Implement `bswap32`: reverse the byte order of a 32-bit value. This converts between
little-endian and big-endian representations — exactly what `htonl`/`ntohl` do under the hood
when sending integers over the network.

## Your task

In `bswap32.c`, implement:

```c
uint32_t bswap32(uint32_t x);
```

Return `x` with its four bytes in reverse order. For example, `0x12345678` becomes
`0x78563412`. **Do not use `__builtin_bswap32`.**

## Hints (no spoilers)

- Isolate each byte with a mask (`x & 0x000000FF`, `x & 0x0000FF00`, ...) and shift it to its
  mirrored position, then OR the four pieces together.
- Byte 0 (lowest) moves to byte 3 (highest): a left shift of 24 bits. Work out the others.
- A correct solution is its own inverse: `bswap32(bswap32(x)) == x`.
